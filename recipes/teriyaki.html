Pork Teriyaki

Pork teriyaki is a delightful dance of flavors on your plate!

Imagine tender, juicy pork marinated in a luscious teriyaki sauce, which is a magical blend of soy sauce, sugar, and a hint of ginger. As it cooks, the sauce caramelizes, creating a sticky glaze that makes every bite irresistible. Whether served over a bed of fluffy rice or tossed with vibrant vegetables in a stir-fry, pork teriyaki is a quick and satisfying dish that feels like a gourmet treat, perfect for any night of the week! üçñ‚ú®

Ingredients

  • Pork tenderloin or pork chops (sliced thin)
  • Soy sauce (low sodium is preferred)
  • Brown sugar (or honey for a healthier option)
  • Mirin (sweet rice wine) or rice vinegar
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Ginger (fresh, grated or minced)
  • Sesame oil (for added flavor)
  • Green onions (sliced, for garnish)
  • Sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)
  • Vegetables (optional, such as bell peppers, broccoli, or snap peas)

Steps to Make Pork Teriyaki

  1. Prepare the Marinade:
    1. In a bowl, mix together soy sauce, brown sugar,
      mirin (or rice vinegar), minced garlic, and grated ginger.
    2. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.
  2. Marinate the Pork:
    1. Place the sliced pork in a resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish.
    2. Pour the marinade over the pork, ensuring it‚Äôs well coated.
    3. Seal the bag or cover the dish and refrigerate for at least minutes to hours
      (or overnight for deeper flavor).
  3. Heat the Pan:
    1. In a large skillet or wok, heat sesame oil over mediumhigh heat.
  4. Cook the Pork:
    1. Remove the pork from the marinade (reserve the marinade) and add it to the hot pan.
    2. Cook until the pork is browned and cooked through, about minutes.
  5. Add the Marinade:
    1. Pour the reserved marinade into the pan.
    2. Bring it to a simmer and let it cook for an additional minutes,
      allowing the sauce to thicken and glaze the pork.
  6. Add Vegetables (Optional):
    1. If using vegetables, add them to the pan during the last few minutes of cooking,
      stirring until they are tendercrisp.
  7. Serve:
    1. Remove from heat and garnish with sliced green onions and sesame seeds (if using).
    2. Serve over steamed rice or noodles.
  8. Enjoy:
    1. Dig into your delicious homemade pork teriyaki and savor the delightful flavors!
    2. Enjoy your cooking adventure! üçñ
